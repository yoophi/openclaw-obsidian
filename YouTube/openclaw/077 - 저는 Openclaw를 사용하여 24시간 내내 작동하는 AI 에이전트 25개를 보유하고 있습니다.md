## Summary
This video demos a custom operations dashboard called Muddy OS that runs a 25‑agent AI team continuously and shows how the creator manages roles, workflows, and memory from one interface. The system uses a centralized task manager (sessions, idle counts, token/cost estimates, cron jobs and logs), an org chart with a CEO and a COO "Muddy" who researches and delegates, and three department heads modeled on familiar public figures for CTO/CMO/CRO responsibilities. *"I have 25 agents running 24/7 autonomously around the clock."*

The creator walks through how agents are organized into divisions (backend/security, frontend/DevOps, QA, content, newsletter, community, product, growth) and which models each subagent uses for specific workloads: Opus 4.6 and Codeex 5.3 for heavy research and code-assist fail-safes, Sonnet 4.5 for content output, Gemini 3 Pro and Gemini 3 Flash for video/motion and community interactions, and smaller models (Nano Banana Pro) for niche tasks. The dashboard exposes transcripts for active sessions, a chron job schedule and overnight logs, and a QA/audit subagent; the creator pairs models for research+output (for example Opus for research and Sonnet for final scripts) and keeps cheaper models dedicated to high-context roles like community because of heavy context feeding. *"Muddy is always available to me. He never cues prompts. He always delegates."*

Finally, the video shows workspace-level identities and persistent memory: one bot (Clay) has a separate workspace, user context and "heartbeat" so it behaves like an independent community member, while Elon/Gary/Warren roles share Muddy’s gateway for orchestration. The creator added voice standups (built with an open‑source voice model), automated a Telegram audio ping after meetings, and built live action-item lists that update when agents mark tasks done; documentation for the ops app updates in real time so agents and humans can reference system changes. The presenter emphasizes iterative improvement, that this is a public build rather than a polished product, and that the system has proven capable of autonomous coordination across many specialized agents.
