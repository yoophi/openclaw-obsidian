## I let an AI CEO run my LowCode agency for two hours

The creator tested Paperclipe, an orchestration platform for “zero human” companies, by giving an AI CEO responsibility for content and letting it spin up agents for specific roles. In about two hours (and $20) the system auto-hired a founding engineer, created a content strategist and content analyzer, produced a content calendar and daily-report tasks, and began parsing recorded sessions to surface post material; *"it decided to hire the founding engineer"* and *"it figured out what we needed to pull from TLV like recordings or transcripts or highlights"*. Agents generated issues and draft posts that required human approval, and the workflow moved very quickly, sometimes feeling opaque.

Operational details: the dashboard shows an org chart (CEO plus three roles in this run), editable agent capabilities, prompt templates, model selection, permissions and budget. The founding engineer connected to TLV via an API key so agents could ingest recordings, transcripts and highlights, then feed that context to the content strategist and analyzer to recommend topics, competitors to watch, creators to follow, and content pillars for LinkedIn, Twitter and a newsletter. The system produced a multiweek content calendar and a daily intelligence report tracking trending topics and top-performing competitors, but the creator cautioned that agent outputs are best when you give clear plans and refined agent prompts.

Conclusions and limits: Paperclipe is powerful for highly agent-driven operations and rapid iteration, but it can feel too autonomous without clearer human-agent collaboration features; the creator wanted roles that mixed AI agents and real humans (developers, QA, approvers) inside the org chart. Overall verdict: promising tool for automating content workflows and code ingestion, useful as a starting point if you supply structure and human oversight.
